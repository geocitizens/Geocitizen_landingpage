About GeoCitizen

GeoCitizen is a digital hub for Community Management, Citizen Participation and Citizen Science. It supports citizen participation processes, community initiatives, citizen science projects and voluntary engagement on different geographic and administrative levels. The core aim of GeoCitizen is to establish a transparent and structured communication process for citizens, municipalities, researchers, economic operators and experts on topics like citizen-oriented development of municipalities, climate change adaptation in smallholder farming, emergency response, revitalization of abandonned urban spaces, amongst others. GeoCitizen supports the collaborative development and realization of projects with communities, academia, public authorities and other stakeholders of planning processes and sustainable projects.

How can GeoCitizen help me?

GeoCitizen is one single tool that can be used in more broadly designed participation processes that are carried out by research institutions, NGOs, municipalities, district organizations or grassroots organizations around the world. Naturally, GeoCitizen is available in different languages. A set of GeoCitizen functionalities are provided to the end-user. Additional modules and functionalities can be easily developed and amended based on APIs if needed in other project environments.

For further information, contact us!

Modules

  • GEO-ENABLED SURVEYS

    Geo-enabled surveys extend standard survey functionalities with features for geographical analysis and visualization. This allows you creating geo-referenced questionnaires and the spatially aggregated presentation of your survey results on easy-to-share digital maps.

  • SURVEY TREES

    Survey Trees help to structure complex surveys through applying a set of logical and mathematic conditions (if-then, etc.). This asissts you in personalizing your questionnaires to the needs of the end users, keeping track of sophisticated issues.

  • CITIZEN ENGAGEMENT

    The Citizen Engagement Module assists you in better managing, structuring and communicating your community projects through map-based reports (issues, ideas, favorite places), discussions, polling and transparent solution-building processes.

  • FACILITATOR-LED SURVEYS

    Facilitator-led surveys support your project team in interviewing persons that are not able to complete a digital survey on their own due to technical or personal restrictions (elderly or handicapped persons, etc).

  • PEER-TO-PEER KNOWLEDGE SHARING

    The Peer-to-Peer Knowledge Sharing module allows users to easily provide specific input and best practice cases to their communities and ask them for feedback in certain fields of interest, building a common knowledge base.

  • PUSH-SMS NOTIFICATIONS

    Push-Notifications assist process managers in keeping their communities up-to-date on the latest developments of their projects and iniciatives, as platform (push) notifications or Short-Message-Service (SMS).

  • Calculator

    After users’ input of requested data, compound variables are calculated following metrics, which are defined by a moderator and provide insights on defined criteria.

  • SKETCH MAPS

    On Sketch Maps, users can share their input on a specific issue or communicate their ideas in a creative way by drawing freehand on a digital map. Results can be easily integrated in further discussions or processes.

  • ALERT NOTIFICATIONS

    Alert Notifications help community leaders and organizations to verify third-party-information on emergency events and therefore better manage emergency response actions in their communities.

  • GDPR-CONFORM MEDIA UPLOAD

    The GDPR-conform media upload automatically verifies if user-generated media input (such as pictures and graphics) on the GeoCitizen-platform meets the EU-GDPR standard.

BÜRGERCOCKPIT ALBERNDORF

Given the example of Alberndorf, the Bürgercockpit is typically included into existent participation processes, for example the development of the future profiles of municipalities of Agenda 21, and therefore supports the sustainable development of projects and initiatives of active citizens, cities and municipalities. Moreover, it offers a transparent platform to (local) economic operators in order to support the development of their ideas (Corporate Social Responsibility).

The Bürgercockpit consists of four sequential modules. In every module a typical section of the participation process is developed:

Module 1 allows creating, designing and evaluating geo-referenced online surveys of specific parameters of quality of life, or example environment, public transportation, security, etc., as well as other questions of interest. The results can be included into the web map of the application as long as these have a spatial component, like dispersed public transportation nodes of different city districts.

Module 2 enables the participants to include their ideas on default topics (e.g. mobility) or categories (e.g. public transportation) and to share these ideas on social media, like Facebook, WhatsApp, Twitter, etc. This can be achieved by georeferenced points (including photos) on the Geocitizen basemap. Topics and categories are designed in way that the constructive proposals and ideas call for an improvement of certain areas of life and do not permit simple complaints.

Module 3 allows for a discussion of specific projects to be implemented based on the preliminary work of Modules 1 and 2. This is carried out in a transparent and structured manner, aiming to jointly develop proposals for specific implementation projects and initiatives, and is usually moderated and accompanied by facilitators.

Module 4 carries out the evaluation/rating of the developed proposals from Module 3 and leads to the realization of project ideas.

In the Geocitzen web app the information generated in Modules 1-4 is brought together and displayed on the basemap as a “traffic light system”. This traffic light reflects the status of the implementation process: Idea > Discussion > Proposal > Rating. Additionally, external information and data (planning foundation, online cadaster, etc.) is included. This way the platform allows presenting a structured, fact-based analysis, discussion and presentation of the participation process in order to reveal typical thematic or spatial patterns, to jointly present ideas of similar processes and to make this publicly available in a transparent manner.

The development of the full cycle of Bürgercockpit-modules will be completed by end of 2019.

GeoFarmer: Smallholder Farmers as Influencers

Users can collect and share geospatial data on weather, farm conditions including soil and crop types, and monitor the adoption of techniques to make farms more productive and resilient to climate change.

The GeoFarmer application was co-developed between the International Center for Tropical Agriculture (CIAT) and the Department of Geoinformatics, University of Salzburg (Z_GIS) and is based on the GeoCitizen framework. It has been extensively tested in Colombia, Ghana, Tanzania and Uganda in order to adapt the GeoCitizen framework for the purpose of research for agricultural development in developing countries.

“An app like this gives farmers a voice and helps close the communication gap between people working in the development sector, researchers working on development technologies, and farmers,” said Andrew Jarvis, director of CIAT’s Decision and Policy Analysis research area. “We’re better at understanding farmers’ needs and we’re more responsive to emerging opportunities and unanticipated challenges”, states Anton Eitzinger from CIAT. Research about the GeoFarmer are published ‘open access’ in Computers and Electronics in Agriculture, Volume 158, March 2019, Pages 109-121.

SE4Amazonian

In SE4Amazonian remote sensing analysis is combined with socio-economic information and participatory mapping to determine the best way to provide clean energy to those who are left behind. Electricity is the gateway to foster other basic services that trigger rural development, such as having light during the night for reading or available energy for charging devices that enable access to information and communication. Working closely with indigenous communities the project is mapping the potentials and needs of clean renewable energy provision for isolated areas. Local technicians and indigenous people are directly involved in the development which will contribute to the success of the initiative. During all project activities it is ensured that sensitive information about involved communities is secured and their rights and wishes respected at all times.

The project was selected as a finalist and won with 11 other project activities the call for ideas to improve the production, management, and use of data in the two thematic areas of “Leave No One Behind” and “the environment” in 2018 by the World Bank´s Development Data group together with the Global Partnership for Sustainable Development Data and is funded by the World Bank Trust Fund for Statistical Capacity Building.

GeoCitizen Atri

The project aims to promote the awareness of young beneficiaries with respect to their double status as Italian and European citizens and to the opportunities that such status entails. In line with those objectives, the project promotes two cycles of workshops for the benefit of 200 students, aged 13-18, attending the secondary education institute in Atri.

The workshops will be animated by the staff of "ARS for Progress of People": NGO with many years of experience in training young people through the use of non-formal education methodologies. During the first cycle of workshops the trainers will propose a game-based methodology that will transmit to the young people key information related to the new European framework for youth policies and to the concepts of active citizenship and structured dialogue. During the second cycle of workshops the young participants will work on the ideation and formulation of proposals and initiatives, in youth policies field, to be submitted to local policy makers.

At the end of the two cycles of workshops the platform "GeoCitizen Atri" will be launched. "GeoCitizen Atri" is an online interactive and geolocalized platform through which all citizens of Atri, aged between 13 and 30, can formulate and submit their proposals to local policy makers. Moreover, through the platform they can comment and vote on the proposals submitted by their peers. The 200 students involved into the two cycles of workshops will contribute to the platform by registering their proposals and will promote the dissemination of the platform to their peers.

At the end of the process the most voted proposals will be presented, during the project "final event”, to 10 policy makers in charge in the municipality of Atri. The 200 students will also attend the final meeting and will discuss with the policy makers about the proposals registered into the platform: identifying positive and negative aspects of each of them. At the end of the day the decision makers will identify the three “most interesting and feasible” proposals and will include them into the local political agenda on youth policies: containing the initiatives to be concretely implemented in the municipal area within the following few months from the project ending date.
